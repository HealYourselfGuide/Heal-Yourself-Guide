The Foundation

The African Bio-Mineral Balance

Alfredo Bowman was born in Honduras in 1933. He never attended medical school. By his own account, he suffered from impotence, obesity, diabetes, asthma, and blindness in one eye — and healed himself through plants. He then studied in Mexico under herbalist Alfredo Cortez, and spent decades travelling through Africa, Latin America, and the Caribbean studying indigenous plant medicine before establishing clinics in New York, Los Angeles, Honduras, and the Caribbean.

He called his methodology the African Bio-Mineral Balance — a system rooted in a single, elegant premise: the body is electrical. It runs on minerals. And when those minerals come from alkaline, natural, non-hybridised plants that the body recognises at the cellular level, healing is not only possible — it is the body's natural state.

"There is only one disease — the inflammation and congestion of the mucous membrane. Everything else is a symptom of it. Feed the body what it recognises. Remove what it doesn't. That is all."
— Dr. Sebi

The Three Core Ideas

I
Disease Is Terrain, Not Attack

Dr. Sebi did not frame disease as an external attacker to be fought. He framed it as a consequence of internal terrain. When the body's internal environment — its pH, its mineral balance, its mucous membrane integrity — is correct, pathogens cannot proliferate and disease cannot take hold. This echoes Béchamp's terrain theory and the broader tradition of naturopathic medicine. It is the opposite of the germ theory framework that governs modern medicine — but it is not incompatible with it. Both can be true simultaneously.

II
Mucus Is the Disease Mechanism

Dr. Sebi's central physiological claim: all disease is rooted in the inflammation and congestion of the mucous membrane. Every organ — lungs, gut, brain, reproductive system — is lined with mucous membrane. When that membrane is compromised by acid-forming foods, processed materials, and chemical residues, it becomes congested and inflamed, preventing nutrient absorption and allowing pathogens to thrive. The alkaline diet removes the conditions that produce excess mucus. The herbs — particularly sea moss, bladderwrack, and burdock root — actively cleanse and restore the mucous membrane.

III
Electric Food = Cellular Recognition

Dr. Sebi distinguished between foods with a complete molecular structure — what he called electric foods — and foods with an incomplete one. Electric foods are those that nature produced without human hybridisation. They grow from seeds that produce seeds. They have the mineral profile and electrical resonance the body was designed to assimilate. Hybrid and GMO foods have been altered to such a degree that the body does not fully recognise them — producing incomplete digestion, inflammatory byproducts, and mucus.

The 1987 New York Supreme Court Case
Dr. Sebi was arrested after running newspaper advertisements claiming he could heal AIDS, cancer, lupus, and other diseases. He was charged with practising medicine without a licence and making fraudulent claims. He appeared in the New York Supreme Court and presented over 70 patients — documented witnesses — who testified to the reversal of their diagnosed conditions under his care. He was acquitted on all counts. The verdict did not validate his science. It validated the documented outcomes. The distinction is important. And the 70 witnesses are a fact that no subsequent narrative about Dr. Sebi ever adequately addresses.
His Celebrity Clients, His Honduras Clinic, and His Death
After his acquittal, his clients included Michael Jackson, Steven Seagal, John Travolta, and Lisa "Left Eye" Lopez, who documented her stay at his Honduras compound on film. His USHA Research Institute became an internationally known destination for people who had exhausted conventional medicine. He died in August 2016 in Honduras, aged 82, under disputed circumstances — officially from pneumonia while in custody on money-laundering charges. He had been arrested at the airport returning to Honduras after speaking engagements in the US. He was treated at the same hospital system whose limitations he had critiqued for decades.

The Non-Negotiables

The Six Laws of the Nutritional Guide

Dr. Sebi's nutritional guide is not a suggestion framework. It is a strict protocol. He was explicit that partial adherence produces partial results — and that the body requires consistency to shift its terrain. These are the core rules.

01
Eat only foods listed in the Nutritional Guide

If a food is not on the list, it is not approved. No exceptions based on how "natural" or "healthy" a food appears. This includes: broccoli (hybrid), spinach (oxalic acid — acidic in the body), seedless grapes, regular bananas (hybridised), most commercial tomatoes (only cherry and plum are approved), and carrots (the common orange variety is hybridised). The list was curated specifically — not arbitrarily.

02
Drink one gallon (3.8 litres) of natural spring water daily

Spring water — not tap water (chlorine, fluoride, pharmaceutical residues), not reverse osmosis (stripped of minerals). Natural spring water carries mineral content that complements the diet. The water requirement is not about hydration alone — it is about continuously flushing the mucus and toxins mobilised by the diet and herbs.

03
No animal products — including dairy and fish

Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy, and honey are all excluded. Dr. Sebi's objection was purely physiological — animal protein creates an acidic internal environment and introduces mucus-forming compounds the body cannot fully process. The approved plant proteins — hemp seeds, walnuts, Brazil nuts, quinoa, spelt, amaranth, wild rice — must be eaten intentionally and consistently.

04
No alcohol

Alcohol is profoundly acid-forming, directly damages the liver, depletes minerals, and impairs cellular repair. No exceptions — including wine, which appears in many "healthy diet" frameworks. The body cannot engage in the cellular repair that the alkaline diet promotes while simultaneously processing alcohol's metabolic byproducts.

05
No microwaves. No canned or seedless fruits

Microwaves alter the molecular structure of food — destroying the electrical resonance that makes electric food therapeutic. Use stovetop or oven only. Canned fruits are processed. Seedless fruits are hybridised — the seed is the indicator of an intact, non-hybridised plant. Seedless = hybrid = incomplete molecular structure in Sebi's framework.

06
Take herbal compounds one hour before pharmaceutical medications

Dr. Sebi did not tell his clients to stop pharmaceutical medications — he directed that his herbs be taken an hour before, so that the body's receptors would be optimally available for the plant compounds. Over time, as the body heals, the need for pharmaceutical support often decreases — but that transition must be managed with a practitioner.

Why Hybrid Foods
The mineral content of hybridised crops versus their heirloom ancestors has been measured. A 2004 study in the Journal of the American College of Nutrition documented reliable declines in protein, calcium, phosphorus, iron, riboflavin, and vitamin C in 43 crops between 1950 and 1999. The mechanism: selective breeding for yield, size, and growth rate consistently dilutes mineral density because mineral content is not the selection criterion. Dr. Sebi's position that the industrialisation of plant breeding has compromised the nutritional quality of common foods is not controversial in the nutritional science literature — only his extension of this into a complete molecular framework is debated.

How to Begin

Transitioning to the Alkaline Protocol

The most common mistake: trying to go fully alkaline overnight. Dr. Sebi himself acknowledged that transition produces discomfort — the body begins releasing stored toxins and mucus as the acid-forming foods are withdrawn, and the cleansing herbs begin their work. This is not illness. But it feels like it, and people quit.

The approach below is not Dr. Sebi's official protocol — he advocated strict adherence from day one. This is a practical transition framework for people who need to build into the lifestyle without the shock of sudden withdrawal from all familiar foods simultaneously.

Wk 1
Begin the water and begin the herbs

Start drinking 1 gallon of spring water daily. Begin sea moss gel (1 tbsp daily) and burdock root tea (1 cup daily). Do not change the diet yet. This allows the drainage pathways to open before dietary change begins mobilising stored toxins. This is the same principle as Phase 1 of the Full Body Detox Protocol — open the exits before you begin clearing the rooms.

Wk 2
Replace breakfast with a fully alkaline meal

Fresh seeded fruit, sea moss smoothie, or a bowl of amaranth porridge with dates and hemp seeds. This one change, sustained for a week, establishes the alkaline morning ritual and begins the metabolic shift without disrupting the entire routine simultaneously.

Wk 3
Remove the biggest offenders

Remove the three most acid-forming items in your current diet: meat, processed wheat (commercial bread and pasta), and dairy. Replace with approved alternatives — wild rice, spelt pasta, quinoa for grains; avocado, hemp seeds, walnuts for protein and fats. This is the largest dietary shift and the one that produces the most significant detox response.

Wk 4+
Full protocol

By week four the body is partially adapted and transition symptoms are reducing. Now: full adherence to the nutritional guide, add the remaining herbs relevant to your specific conditions, and deepen the water intake. At this stage the protocol is self-sustaining — the foods become familiar, the herbs become routine, and the body's response to alkaline eating becomes its own motivation.

The Cornerstone

How to Make Sea Moss Gel

The most important daily practice in the Dr. Sebi protocol. 1–2 tablespoons of raw sea moss gel delivers a concentrated dose of 92 minerals directly to the body. It can be made at home in 24 hours for a fraction of the cost of pre-made products.

Raw Sea Moss Gel — Basic Recipe
100g dried Irish sea moss (raw, not processed)
Spring water — enough to soak
Key lime juice — ½ lime (optional, acts as preservative)
Glass storage jar
Rinse the dried sea moss thoroughly under cool water to remove salt and debris.
Soak in a bowl of spring water for 12–24 hours. The moss will expand 3–4× its dry size and become gelatinous. Change the water once midway through.
Drain and rinse again. Add the soaked moss to a blender with enough fresh spring water to cover (approximately 2 cups per 100g). Blend on high for 60 seconds until completely smooth and gel-like.
Add the lime juice if using. Pour into a glass jar. Refrigerate — will set to a firm gel within 2 hours.
Use 1–2 tablespoons daily — add to smoothies, drinks, or eat directly. Keeps refrigerated for up to 3 weeks.

Daily Alkaline Routine

A full day on the Dr. Sebi alkaline protocol — combined with the broader healing practices from this guide. Every component serves the core goal: alkalinity, mineral replenishment, mucous membrane restoration.

On Waking
2 glasses of natural spring water — before anything else. The body is dehydrated after sleep and the kidneys are ready to flush. Begin the 1-gallon daily count from here.
Morning Ritual
Sea moss smoothie — 1 tbsp sea moss gel + 1 cup spring water + burro banana + dates + hemp seeds + bladderwrack powder. Blend. This is the foundational mineral dose for the day. Add elderberry or burdock root tea alongside.
Breakfast
Seeded fruit plate — papaya, mango, berries, figs. Or amaranth porridge with dates, walnuts, hemp seeds, and agave. Let fruit settle before cooked food if eating both.
Mid-Morning
Herbal tea — burdock root, chamomile, or mullein (for respiratory conditions). Continue spring water throughout the morning. Sarsaparilla tincture if working on blood and hormonal restoration.
Lunch
Grain bowl — wild rice or quinoa base + avocado + cherry tomatoes + dandelion greens or kale + olives + grapeseed oil + pure sea salt + herbs. Or spelt pasta with homemade cherry/plum tomato and herb sauce. The largest meal of the day.
Afternoon

Dinner
Lighter than lunch — large green salad (wild arugula, watercress, cucumber, avocado, hemp seeds, key lime dressing). Or vegetable soup with approved grains. End eating at least 3 hours before bed.
Evening
Final herbal tea — chamomile or lemon grass. Remaining daily spring water. Dr. Sebi described the hours 10pm–2am as healing hours — the body's primary repair window. Be asleep by 10pm when possible.
